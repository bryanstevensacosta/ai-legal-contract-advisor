## Team Collaboration Guidelines

### Communication
- **Slack/Discord**: Primary communication channel during hackathon
- **Response Time**: Best effort during work hours (reasonable expectations)
- **Availability**: Communicate your availability clearly to the team
- **Time Zones**: Be mindful of team members in different time zones

### Work Distribution
- **Fair division** of tasks based on skills and interests
- **Transparency** about progress and blockers
- **Help when needed** - no one should struggle alone
- **Flexibility** to adjust roles as needed

### Decision Making
- **Consensus preferred** for major decisions
- **Team Lead** has final say if consensus can't be reached
- **Document decisions** in project files or chat
- **Respect the decision** once made and move forward

### Conflict Resolution
- **Address issues directly** and respectfully with the person involved
- **Assume good intentions** - most conflicts are misunderstandings
- **Focus on solutions** rather than blame
- **Escalate to Team Lead** if direct resolution doesn't work
- **Keep it private** - don't air conflicts publicly

---

## Hackathon-Specific Guidelines

### Compliance
- **Follow IBM hackathon rules** at all times
- **Use only allowed data sources** (no PII, client data, etc.)
- **Protect credentials** - never commit API keys
- **Respect submission deadline** - Feb 1, 2026, 10:00 AM ET
- **Be honest** about our work and contributions

### Intellectual Property
- **All work is team property** during the hackathon
- **Respect third-party licenses** for any code/libraries used
- **Document sources** for any external code or ideas
- **Team owns the submission** collectively

### External Interactions
When representing Team AI Kings:
- **Be professional** in Slack channels and with mentors
- **Be respectful** when asking for help
- **Give back** by helping other teams when possible
- **Promote positive image** of our team and IBM

## Scope

## Enforcement

### Reporting
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:
1. **Address directly** with the person if you feel comfortable
2. **Contact Team Lead** privately if direct resolution doesn't work
3. **Contact IBM hackathon organizers** for serious violations

### Consequences
Depending on severity, violations may result in:
- **Warning** and request to change behavior
- **Temporary removal** from team communications
- **Removal from team** (for serious or repeated violations)
- **Report to hackathon organizers** (for rule violations)

### Privacy
- Reports will be handled with discretion
- Only involved parties and Team Lead will be informed
- Focus is on resolution, not punishment
